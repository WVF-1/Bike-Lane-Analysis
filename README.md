# Bike-Lane-Analysis
A look into how bike lanes have evolved and appeared over time in Pittsburgh, where they are present, and who uses them.

## Data Sources

- **Street network & bike infrastructure**: © OpenStreetMap contributors, available under the
  [Open Database License (ODbL)](https://opendatacommons.org/licenses/odbl/). Data accessed
  programmatically via the [`osmnx`](https://github.com/gboeing/osmnx) Python library
  (Boeing, G. 2017. "OSMnx: New Methods for Acquiring, Constructing, Analyzing, and
  Visualizing Complex Street Networks." *Computers, Environment and Urban Systems* 65,
  126–139).

- **Bike commute share & tract boundaries**: U.S. Census Bureau, *American Community Survey,
  2022 5-Year Estimates*, Table B08301 (Means of Transportation to Work). Tract boundaries
  from the U.S. Census Bureau's TIGER/Line Shapefiles, accessed via the
  [`pygris`](https://github.com/walkerke/pygris) Python library. Public domain (U.S.
  federal government data).
